Impressionism Paintings.

Impressionism is an art movement that emerged in the late 19th century, primarily in France. It is characterized by its focus on capturing the fleeting effects of light, color, and atmosphere in a spontaneous and subjective manner. Impressionist paintings often feature visible brushstrokes, vibrant colors, and an emphasis on the play of light and shadow.

Impressionist painters sought to depict the ever-changing qualities of nature and everyday scenes, rather than aiming for a precise representation of reality. They were particularly interested in capturing the momentary impressions and sensations of a scene, rather than the meticulous details. This approach marked a departure from the academic conventions of the time.
