Abstract Art.

Abstract art is a genre of visual art that does not attempt to represent an accurate depiction of visual reality. Instead, it emphasizes the use of colors, shapes, lines, and forms to create a visual language that is independent of any recognizable objects or figures. The main focus of abstract art is to evoke emotions, sensations, or ideas through the arrangement of these non-representational elements.

Abstract art emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a response to the changing societal and cultural landscape. Artists began to challenge the traditional notions of art, seeking new ways to express their inner visions and explore the boundaries of artistic expression.
